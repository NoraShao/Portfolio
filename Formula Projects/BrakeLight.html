FSAE Car Brake Light

September 2022 - April 2023

In my first project on the Formula team, I was responsible for designing and manufacturing a brake light for the car.

I started by reviewing my project goals. The FSAE rules contained guidelines for the LED colour and sizing, so I had an approximate idea of how many LEDs I would need. As well, I had to consider the brake light's integration with the rest of the racecar. For example, it had to be IP-67 rated and actuated by a preset 12V brake signal. With this information in mind, I determined appropriate resistor-LED values. To select the LEDs used on the actual brake light, I started by designing an LED test board.

I designed the PCB schematic and layout in Altium Designer.

Once I selected my LEDs, I worked on the actual brake light PCB. Some of the considerations I had to make included heat dissipation.

In addition to manufacturing the brake light PCB, I was also responsible for protecting it from environmental factors and mounting it on the car. I designed a 3D-printable resin enclosure in Solidworks that could be mounted onto the rear differentials of the car and would also act as a diffuser for the LEDs. Before I secured the PCB in its water and airtight enclosure, for additional water resistance, I applied a layer of conformal coating onto the PCB itself.
